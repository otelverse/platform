# OTelVerse Governance

This document describes how the OTelVerse project is governed, how decisions are made, and how contributors can become maintainers.

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Community Members
Anyone who uses OTelVerse, participates in discussions, reports issues, or provides feedback is a community member.

### Contributors
Community members who submit pull requests, improve documentation, help answer questions, or actively assist others in the community.

### Maintainers
Maintainers are active contributors who have demonstrated a deep understanding of the project and a commitment to its success. Maintainers have write access to the repository, can merge pull requests, and cut releases.

## Decision Making Process

Decisions in the OTelVerse project are generally made through **lazy consensus**. 
When a change is proposed (e.g., via a Pull Request or Issue), maintainers and contributors review and discuss it. If there are no objections within a **48-hour window**, the change is considered approved.

For major architectural changes, API-breaking changes, or significant project direction shifts, a formal vote among the Maintainers may be required.

## Becoming a Maintainer

We welcome new maintainers! To become a maintainer, a contributor should:
1. Make consistent and high-quality contributions to the project over a period of at least 3 months.
2. Be nominated by an existing maintainer.
3. Be approved by the current maintainers with no strong objections.

## Conflict Resolution

If a technical or process dispute cannot be resolved through regular discussion and consensus, the issue will be escalated to the Lead Maintainer for a final decision. 

## Community Meetings

We hold community calls every 2 weeks to discuss the roadmap, demonstrate new features, and gather feedback. Attendance is optional but highly encouraged for regular contributors and maintainers. Details are posted in our Discord server and GitHub Discussions.
